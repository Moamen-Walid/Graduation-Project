Benefits of vegetables and fruits

  • Vegetables and fruits play a major and essential role in maintaining human health. Their nutrient content varies depending on their colors and shapes and the way they are treated during cultivation in terms of strengthening their soil. And the water used to irrigate it. Therefore, we must be careful to diversify its intake in order to obtain the greatest possible benefits from it.

  • Carotenoids:

  • are one of the widespread natural food pigmentswhich are usually found in orange, yellow or red vegetables and fruits, in addition to To green leafy vegetables.
  • Carotenoids add a distinctive color to our food and provide it with many health benefits.They are found in several forms,including :-
  • Carotenoids are considered a form of vitamin A and are a good source of it, as carotenoids are transformed within the body into vitamin A, which is considered an essential vitamin for growth, reproduction and functions. immune system. It is found incarrots, sweet potatoes, apricots, mango, nectarines, oranges, papaya, persimmons, pineapples, tomatoes. And spinach.
  • Lutein and xanthin are very important for eye health. The most prominent food sources that provide us with them are:cantaloupe, corn, carrots, yellow and orange peppers, fish, salmon and eggs Lycopene, found in tomatoes, may reduce the risk of prostate, ovarian, stomach, and pancreatic cancers.

  • Flavonoids:

  • Flavonoids are considered one of the plant pigments that give plant flowers their colors.They work as antioxidants, anti-cancer, and protect against coronary heart disease.

  • Recommended amounts of vegetables and fruits:

  • Modern recommendations indicate the importance of eating at least five servings of vegetables and fruits daily, provided that the vegetables are from the. The group of starchy vegetables includes corn, peas, and potatoes.
  • The size of one serving of vegetables is as follows :
  • 1/2 cup of green vegetables, or orange vegetables (such as spinach, carrots).
  • 1/2 cup kidney beans (cooked, dried or canned).
  • 1/2 cup of lentils.
  • 1 cup of leafy vegetables.
  • 1 medium tomato.
  • Examples of one serving size of fruits include:
  • 1 medium apple, banana, orange or pear.
  • 2 small apricots, plums or kiwis.
  • 1/2 cup of natural juice.
  • 1 cup of chopped or canned fruit without sugar.
