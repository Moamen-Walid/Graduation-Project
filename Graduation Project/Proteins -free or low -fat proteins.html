The Proteins are necessary to build and repair tissues.

  • The Proteins are the building blocks of the body and are vital macronutrients for maintaining and repairing tissues, cells, and organs. Despite its presence in the foods we eat every day, many of us still fall short of consuming the required amount. By understanding what lean protein is best, an individual can increase their protein intake without worrying about excess fat.
  • Choose the best lean protein in terms of type and quantity :
  • When ingested, the body breaks down protein into 20 amino acids necessary for growth. Many protein sources, whether eggs, meat, poultry, fish, or meat, contain all the amino acids that the body needs. Protein sources such as: moisture, grains, beans, and nuts are often sourced to one or more essential amino compositions. However, this does not mean that animal protein is a must. Protein can be increased by including a variety of plant sources.
  • Adults should eat at least 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. While breastfeeding women should aim to get about 20 grams more of high-quality protein per day than before pregnancy to support lactation, older adults should aim for 1 to 1.5 grams of protein per kilogram of weight.
  • Protein quality :
  • Checking the types and quality of protein before consuming it is crucial. For example, both red meat and full-fat dairy products are rich in protein, but contain saturated fat.
  • One way to identify the best protein sources is to examine meat that is industrially raised with high-quality ex-grass-fed organic meat. You should always choose high-quality sources when trying to increase your protein intake.
  • How to get the best lean protein :

1– Legumes, beans, and lentils are low-fat sources of protein and other nutrients. It does not provide all the amino acids or protein components that the body needs. However, several types of grains can be combined to provide the body with the required amino acids.

2- Chicken breasts, turkey, oysters, and most types of fish are excellent sources of protein. Removing the skin from these sources reduces the fat content significantly.

3- Choose seafood three times a week, because it is rich in protein and low in saturated fat. Salmon, sardines, anchovies and herring are also rich in omega-3 fatty acids.

4- Meat from grass-fed animals contains a lower percentage of fat compared to industrial meat.

5- Choose the best cooking methods, such as grilling, boiling, or steaming, to reduce fat.

6- Processed meats are a good source of protein, but they are often loaded with salt. This can lead to high blood pressure and other health problems. Therefore it should be avoided.

7- Skip processed cheese because it often contains non-dairy ingredients.

8- Snack on nuts and seeds instead of chips, replace baked goods with Greek yogurt, or replace pizza slices with grilled chicken breast and beans. Always choose unsalted nuts and seeds to reduce your sodium intake.

9- Tofu (a type of cheese) can help you meet your protein needs and does not contain a lot of fat.

10- Soybeans are also an excellent alternative to red meat, and are considered the best low-fat protein and a good addition to vegetarian menus.

11- Adding vegetables such as spinach, peas, and mushrooms increases the protein content. They are also fat-free and rich in good fibre, making them a great addition to your meals.

12- Increase protein in your diet by replacing processed carbohydrates with high-quality protein. Avoid cakes, pastries, biscuits, chips and pizza and replace them with white meat, fish, beans, nuts, seeds, peas, dairy products and tofu. This will help you maintain a healthy weight and reduce your risk of heart disease and stroke.

  • The best types of low-calorie, high-protein vegetables and meat:
1-Tuna :
  • Seafood is a great source of dietary protein because it is one of the best low-calorie options. Tuna is widely available in canned form. Fish provides about 20 grams of protein in less than 100 calories (per 100 grams), depending on the type of tuna you use. Tuna is a source of lean protein and is good for the heart as well.
2-Turkey :
  • 100 grams of turkey breast contains only 157 calories with 22 grams of protein, making it the best lean, low-calorie protein.
3-Quraysh cheese :
  • If you're a vegetarian and looking for a low-calorie, high-protein food, it doesn't get any better than this – a 100g portion of cottage cheese contains 98 calories and 11g of protein.
4-Eggs :
  • Chicken eggs are one of the most popular and cheapest proteins. 100 grams of chicken eggs contain 155 calories and 13 grams of protein.
5-Spinach :
  • If you need some good vegetables to accompany your meat, spinach should be your best choice. Spinach contains about 20 calories per 100 grams, along with approximately 3 grams of protein.
6-Salmon :
  • Salmon can add to your protein intake without breaking your calorie budget. Depending on the type of salmon, 100 grams of fish provides between 18 and 25 grams of protein, all within 200 calories.
7-Lentils :
  • Lentils are an edible legume that are a very popular source of protein among vegetarians. Lentils are very low in calories and rich in protein. 100 grams of boiled lentils contain 9 grams of protein, with only 116 calories.
8-Peas :
  • Green peas are also a protein-rich vegetable food that you should add to your daily diet. 100 grams of raw green peas contain 81 calories and 5 grams of protein.
9-Yogurt :
  • Yogurt is also a great low-calorie, high-protein option for those watching their weight. A 100 gram portion of yoghurt contains 98 calories and 11 grams of protein.
10-Mushrooms :
  • Mushrooms are another great vegetable that you can add to a chicken or turkey sandwich or salad. Mushrooms contain 3.1 grams of protein per 100 grams, with only 22 calories.
